The Seattle Supersonics

A Team Given Up On

Timeline

  • - Founded on December 20, 1966 by Los Angeles businessman Sam Schulman and Eugene V Klein
  • - Achieved their first winning season after only two years
  • - Made their first NBA Finals during the 1977-78 season but fell short in seven games to the Washington Bullets
  • - Returned the next year and beat the Bullets in Seven games to win their first NBA Championship
  • - Schulman sold the team to Barry Ackerley in the 1980's which led to a decline
  • - Drafted Shawn Kemp in 1989 and Gary Payton in 1990
  • - Became one of the best teams throughout the 1990's making the finals in 1996 only to lose to the 72-10 Chicago Bulls
  • - Suffered their first losing season in 11 years after trading Gary Payton in 2002
  • - Sold to the Professional Basketball Club LLC led by Oklahoma City Businessman Clay Bennett in 2006
  • - Due to a lack of support from the Washington Government to remodel the Sonics home arena, Bennett relocated the team to Oklahoma City in 2008
  • - Fans felt betrayed by the ownership and city which has been without a team since
